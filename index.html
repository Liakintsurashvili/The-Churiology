Georgia, The Cradle of Wine

Georgia boasts more than 8,000 vintages and some of the world’s most fascinating winemaking traditions.Natural wines, amber wines, amphorae, fruit-forward reds, terroir-driven wines, indigenous grape varieties – Georgia seems to have it all.

Georgia is considered by many to be the birthplace of wine, and its history can be traced back over 8000 years when the people of the South Caucasus discovered that if wild grape juice was buried in a pit over winter, it turned into wine. This led to Georgians cultivating grapes and burying clay vessels, or kvevris/churi, to create wine - sometimes for as long as 50 years for a true vintage. Archaeologists have found these kveris in a huge variety of different shapes, sizes and styles, often with a highly skilled level of craftmanship, highlighting how much wine was revered by ancient Georgians. Kveris are not just a piece of Georgian wine history, they are still used in Georgian wine production today (see below) and this process has been given heritage protection status by UNESCO.
